Spaced repetition — the evidence

Conversation2 turnsgrounded in 24 sources
What's the strongest evidence we have that spaced repetition beats massed practice?
14m ago

The clearest signal in your notes is Cepeda's 2006 meta-analysis, which pooled 254 studies and found a consistent advantage for distributed practice across nearly every condition tested1. The effect grows with the retention interval — the longer you need to remember something, the more spacing helps2.

Mechanistically, your reading ties this back to Ebbinghaus: each successful retrieval after partial forgetting resets the decay curve from a higher floor, so the same total study time buys more durable memory when it's spread out3.

Does that hold for procedural skills, or just facts?
9m ago

Mostly, but the picture is thinner. Your motor-learning notes show spacing still helping for procedural skills — typing, surgery simulations, music — though the gains are smaller and more sensitive to how the sessions are structured4.

One caveat worth flagging: a couple of sources argue that for continuous motor tasks, massed blocks can look better during training before spacing wins out at a later test5. You don't yet have a settled claim reconciling the two — it's an open thread.
